# Concepts

This document defines the public terminology of the FlowAxion Business Reality Engine.

## FlowAxion

FlowAxion is an AI-native business operating concept designed to synthesize company reality into operational memory, workflows, interfaces, agents, and concrete outputs.

## Business Reality Engine

The Business Reality Engine is the macro-system that transforms the real-world context of a company into structured operational intelligence.

It connects physical, document-based, human, operational, and market context.

## Reality-to-Action Engine

The Reality-to-Action Engine is the execution concept that turns structured company memory into concrete actions, such as tasks, documents, workflows, websites, proposals, dashboards, reminders, and strategic outputs.

## Business Memory

Business Memory is the structured memory of the company.

It can include identity, assets, services, products, documents, people, processes, customers, competitors, risks, opportunities, and pending actions.

Business Memory should preserve confidence levels and validation states.

## Field Scan

Field Scan is the video-first acquisition flow where a business owner can record the real environment of the company and let AI generate an initial operational understanding.

Examples:

- warehouse scan;
- equipment scan;
- shop scan;
- office scan;
- vehicle and tool scan;
- document and signage scan.

## The Synthesizer

The Synthesizer is the core FlowAxion principle.

It means transforming scattered company reality into structured memory and action.

## Co-design Studio

Co-design Studio is the collaborative layer where owner, AI, and collaborators shape business outputs together.

It can be used for websites, workflows, offers, operational processes, documents, and strategy.

## Atomic Blueprint

Atomic Blueprint is the structured representation concept that connects business knowledge, UI elements, workflows, rules, and outputs.

The detailed implementation is private and not published in this repository.

## Validation states

FlowAxion should distinguish between:

1. **Observed**: detected or inferred by AI.
2. **Confirmed**: confirmed by the business owner or authorized user.
3. **Validated**: supported by a document, evidence, record, or trusted source.

This prevents generic AI output from being treated as operational truth.
